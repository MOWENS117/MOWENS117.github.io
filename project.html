Project Information

Project Aims

This project investigates the relationship between Bitcoin's market dynamics and its environmental impact through energy consumption patterns. The primary objectives were to:

Analyze the correlation between Bitcoin's price movements and its energy consumption trends, examining whether increased market value leads to proportional increases in energy usage. Additionally, we aimed to identify any potential decoupling between price and energy consumption that might suggest improvements in mining efficiency or shifts in mining practices.

Furthermore, the project sought to understand the long-term sustainability implications of Bitcoin's growing energy demands and evaluate whether the network's energy consumption shows signs of stabilization or continues to grow unbounded with price appreciation.

Data Sources & Methodology

The analysis combines data from multiple authoritative sources:

Bitcoin price and volume data was sourced from the Coin Gecko, providing historical daily price points from 2013 to present. Energy consumption data was collected from the Cambridge Bitcoin Electricity Consumption Index (CBECI), which offers a real-time estimate of Bitcoin's electricity consumption.
